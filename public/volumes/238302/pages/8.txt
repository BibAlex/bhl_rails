Coccoloba liportizii is closely related to Coccoloba tenui -
folia L. from Jamaica, Cuba and Bahama, but differs from it in
having stipules (ocreae) bearing 2 tufts of hairs, petioles and
midrib quite glabrous, leaves acuminate, rachis glabrous and
fruits bigger. Another unusual feature of this species is its
restriction to premontane rain forest formations on the Cari-
bbean slope. It is the first Coccoloba to have been found gro-
wing wild above 1000 meters elavation in Costa Rica. (Cf. Bur-
ger in Fieldiana, Bot. n. s. 13 : 112. 1983).
