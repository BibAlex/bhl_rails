134 BROWN SWALLOW.
caudacuta ; it being much larger, and differing in
other particulars from that bird : its length is six
inches and a half, breadth about twenty : its beak
and legs are dusky : the forehead is pale : the
prevailing colour of the plumage is dusky brown,
the head and neck deepest, and tinged with green :
middle of the back light brown : quills and tail deep
brown green ; the greater quills black on the outer
edges : under parts of the body dark brown : throat
and chin whitish: vent and under tail-coverts
white. Native place unknown.
