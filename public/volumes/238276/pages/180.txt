AMERICAN LUMBER IN FOREIGN MARKETS.
CLIMATE AND GENERAL BUILDING.
The climate in this part of France is mild, though the winters may
be considered somewhat damp and rainy, and sometimes even rigor-
ous ; the summers, on the contrary, are dry and hot.
Although the Garonne Eiver, on which Bordeaux is situated, is a
fine, broad tidal waterway, the shipbuilding industry does not flour-
ish here. There does exist a shipbuilding establishment for the con-
struction of war vessels, but these, it is needless to say, are built of
steel.
My advice to American exporters would be to send a representative
to this city to study the condition of the market and offer the home
material at somewhat more advantageous prices than those paid for
European lumber used here. This could be readily done, and still
leave a wide profit for the American shipper.
