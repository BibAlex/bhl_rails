SUMMARY.
If we summarize the results of this chapter, we arrive at the following
conclusions : —
i. With at least 180 cm. of rainfall, the high-forest alone predominates.
In regard to rainfalls of 150-180 cm. no data are available.
1. With 90-150 cm. of rainfall there is a struggle between xerophilous
woodland and grassland. Xerophilous woodland gains the victory when
greater heat and more prolonged rainless periods prevail during the
vegetative season ; grassland succeeds when a milder temperature, a more
even distribution of rainfall during the vegetative season, and windy dry
or frosty seasons prevail.
3. With a rainfall below 90 cm., xerophilous scrub, in particular thorn-
forest and thorn-bush, prevails; both of these, if the precipitation be less,
pass over into open scrub (semi-desert).
