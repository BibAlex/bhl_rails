SENECIO DANYAUSII LOBATIFOLIUS Homb. & Jacq.
Leaves serrate or pinnatifid.
E. Fuegia (Dusen).
S. DANYAUSII PINNATIFIDUS Macl., n. var.
Very like S. danyatisii lobatifolius, except that the leaves are somewhat
more deeply serrate or pinnatifid. (Determined by Dr. O. Hoffmann.)
By Hatcher at Rio Sta. Cruz, S. Patagon., Jan., 1897.
21. S. DARWINII Hook. & Arn.
Heads radiate. Low, prostrate shrubs, much branching. Leaves
woolly, much as in 5. falklandiciis, but generally cuneate and trifid or tri-
lobate or repand-dentate. Peduncles long, or heads sessile among the
upper leaves.
Magellan, Fuegia.
S. DARWINII ERADIATUS Gray.
Low, condensed, leaves small. Ligules none.
Fuegia, at Orange Harbor.
22. S. DELFINI Phil.
Corymb lax, many-headed. Tall, glabrous herb, with simple, fistulous
stem. Leaves pinnatifid, to 9 pairs, the segments narrow-linear. Lower
peduncles very long, naked or i -leaved, 3-5 headed, with bracteolate
pedicels. Heads 6 mm. in diameter, non-calyculate ; invohicral scales 10.
Achenes white silky.
Valley of Rio Palena, west of the Cordillera.
23. S. DESIDERATUS DC.
Subcorynibose, few-headed. Glabrous undershrub, with terete branches
and sessile, pinnatipartite leaves, having linear rachis, and 6-8 pairs of seg-
ments, these acute and undivided or bipartite to their base ; the uppermost
linear, entire. Floriferous branches bracteate. Involucre ovate, calycu-
late, its scales about 10, acute, non-sphacelate. Ligules 10, many-nerved;
disk-flowers 25-30. Achenes slightly hairy.
S. Patagon., by Puerto Deseado ; Magellan, Golfo de San Jorge.
