Remarks. — -The difference between the type specimens of volcanica
Butler and mimica Felder is very minimal and merely consists of the
confluence of three terminal streaks of the forewing and a somewhat
distinct shape of the apical patch of the hindwing in mimica. These
characters are rather inconstant and vary from specunen to specimen.
In general, the subspecies volcanica seems to be less variable than the
subspecies rivularis, and no forms with the streaks of the forewing
widely reduced or confluent as described in the latter subspecies are
known for the subspecies volcanica.
